Zambuko (born April 23, 2015) is an EASA biologist and astronaut.

Background

Zambuko was born on April 23, 2015, in Zebrica. Fascinated by the natural sciences from a young age, Zambuko pursued their studies at the University of Zebrica, graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Biology with a focus on extraterrestrial ecosystems. After obtaining their degree in 2038, they joined the Zebrican Biological Research Institute, focusing on cellular adaptation under varying gravitational conditions.

EASA

In December 2038, Zambuko was selected to join EASA Astronaut Group 42, following a collaborative program between EASA and Zebrica's Space Agency. Known for their expertise in biological adaptation, they were chosen as a key scientific member of the Stardust 21 mission, tasked with studying microbial resilience in deep space conditions.

Personal life

Zambuko is known for their deep connection with the wilderness of Zebrica, often spending their downtime exploring the flora and fauna of their homeland. In addition to their scientific pursuits, they have advocated for sustainable research practices and serve as an inspiration to many young zebras interested in space sciences.
