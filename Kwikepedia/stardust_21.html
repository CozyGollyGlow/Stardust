Stardust 21 is a manned space mission organized by the Equestrian Aerospace and Space Administration (EASA) in coordination with other space agencies (such as KIRA), primarily focused on the maintenance and upgrade of the MIDST (Multi-Imaging Deep Space Telescope), the largest space telescope deployed by EASA, as well as additional scientific research activities.

Mission Overview

Scheduled for launch on April 22, 2045, from the Jetstream Space Launch Centre, Stardust 21 represents a major milestone in Equestrian interplanetary missions. The mission will last approximately 12 months and will focus on servicing and refitting the MIDST telescope, critical for deep-space imaging and astronomical research.

Crew Members

The Stardust 21 crew consists of five selected astronauts, each with specialized training to ensure successful mission outcomes:
  • Rosetta (Captain, EASA) - The mission leader overseeing MIDST operations and ensuring mission success, Rosetta is responsible for leading and coordinating the team.
  • Concorde (Pilot, EASA) - The mission’s skilled pilot, Concorde manages navigation, positioning the spacecraft for optimal access to the MIDST telescope.
  • Aurora (Engineer, KSA) - Responsible for maintaining and repairing the telescope’s systems, Aurora is tasked with the detailed refit and equipment upgrades for MIDST.
  • Zambuko (Biologist, ZSA) - Conducting biological research related to the mission’s long-duration space travel, Zambuko performs experiments on cellular and microbial adaptation.
  • Wolfram (Medical Expert, GSA) - The mission’s doctor, Wolfram monitors crew health, studying the physiological effects of prolonged space exposure, and providing in-flight medical care as needed.

Launch Site

The mission will launch from the Jetstream Space Launch Centre in Trottingham, Equestria, located on the eastern coast of the Shetland Islands. Known for its advanced launch capabilities, the center provides critical support for deep-space mission trajectories.

Objectives

The objectives of the Stardust 21 mission include:
  • To conduct extensive repairs and upgrades to the MIDST telescope, enhancing its imaging capabilities and extending its operational life
  • To perform essential maintenance tasks on MIDST’s solar array and communications systems
  • To carry out biological research on microbial resilience and cellular adaptation to space conditions
  • To collect data on the physical and psychological effects of prolonged space travel on the crew
